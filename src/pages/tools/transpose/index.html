Shift every note and chord in your text up or down by a number of semitones. Naturals like G, sharps like F#, flats like Bb, and chords like Cmaj7 or D/F# are moved — lyrics, spacing, and everything else stay exactly as written.

What moves Note letters, accidentals (#/b, ♯/♭), chord qualities (m, maj7, sus4, dim, aug…), the bass of slash chords, and runs of notes packed together like CD#F.

Load an example  ·  A word is transposed only if it's a valid chord (Cmaj7) or reads entirely as notes, so Bed, cabbage and Go are left alone. Because adjacent notes are now split, two things do shift by design: a lone capital A (it's the note A), and all-caps words built only from the letters A–G — e.g. FACE or BAG — since those are indistinguishable from packed-note notation.
